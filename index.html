I · The Brand

A mark that reads as a private firm, not another marketing agency.

You're selling to insurance agents who've been through ten spray-and-pray vendors. The visual identity has to say operator, not growth hacker. The proposed direction leans editorial and financial — closer to a private banking brochure than a SaaS landing page.

Tone

  • BeDirect and sales-forward. Speak like a closer who's already run the math. No soft pedaling. No "let's partner up" filler.
  • BeData-backed. Every claim gets a number next to it. CPA, LTV, close rates, lead volume.
  • BePersonal. Your face, your voice, your story. The agent is buying you, not a logo.
  • AvoidGeneric "digital marketing" vocabulary ("synergy," "empower," "unlock"). We're not a deck.
  • AvoidStock photography, gradients of purple, and any hero that shows people in suits shaking hands.
II · The Positioning

What HMS is. And what it isn't.

The mistake most new agencies make is pitching the whole kitchen. A founder who sells meta ads, SEO, VAs, cold calling, and data all at once gets filtered as "yet another generalist." The positioning below locks in a vertical, a geography, and a promise — then expands on proof, not hope.

HMS is
  • YesA Florida-first lead partner for final-expense life-insurance agents.
  • YesA full-stack delivery team — creative, media buying, landing pages, CRM, VA follow-up — under one roof.
  • YesA proprietary intent-data layer (via ArkData / ListMagic) that no other final-expense lead vendor runs.
  • YesA future agency, with Ethan licensed, taking overrides on the business he creates.
HMS is not
  • NoA home services agency. Not roofers, not landscapers, not HVAC. That fight is lost on price.
  • NoA general ad agency. We will turn down jobs outside the ICP for the first twelve months.
  • NoA BPO storefront. The overseas team is fulfillment muscle, not the hero of the pitch.
  • NoA Runday side project. This is a separate brand with its own P&L.

VI · The Vertical Rollout

Something is always in season.

The strategy isn't to pick one vertical and hope. It's to stack verticals so that agent clients acquired in Phase 1 are natural upsells into Phase 2, 3, and 4 — and to ride seasonal spikes (AEP, hurricane, OEP) without ever having a quiet quarter.

Phase 1 · Now
Final Expense
Year-round demand, proven lead culture, fastest proof of concept.
Phase 2 · Mo 2–3
Home & Flood
Florida-specific urgency. Hurricane season starts June. Citizens crisis = urgency.
Phase 3 · Q4
Medicare (AEP)
Oct 15 start. Massive agent spend window. Stack onto existing FE book.
Phase 4 · Ongoing
Auto & ACA
Volume plays. Fills capacity, diversifies revenue base.
Phase 5 · When ready
Commercial & Annuities
Premium upsell for top agents. Highest per-lead price, stickiest LTV.
