# Final Expense Market Snapshot — Sourced Data

Compiled April 2026. All figures cited inline. Where a number is an estimate built from multiple sources, the math is shown. The "FE-target population" used throughout is defined as: residents age 65+ in households where the householder's annual income falls under $75,000. Lower-to-middle-income share is anchored to LIMRA/Pension Rights Center data showing that median income for Americans 65+ was $33,310 in 2024 and that ~75–80% of households headed by someone 65+ fall under $75,000 in annual income.

## A. State-Level Final-Expense Market Sizing — Top 20 States

**Methodology.** Top 20 states selected by total population (2025 estimates). Population age 65+ taken from CarePatrol's compilation of 2023 ACS 5-year estimates (cross-checked vs. PRB and Census Bureau figures). The "FE-target population" multiplies the state's 65+ population by **0.78** — a conservative national share of 65+ households with annual income under $75,000, derived as follows:

- Median income for Americans 65+ was $33,310 in 2024 ([ACL Profile of Older Americans, 2024](https://acl.gov/aging-and-disability-in-america/data-and-research/profile-older-americans))
- Half of older households received less than $56,680 in 2024 ([Pension Rights Center, 2024](https://pensionrights.org/resource/income-of-todays-older-adults/))
- Just over 50% of all US households had income under $75,000 in 2023; the share for 65+ households is meaningfully higher because of retirement and reduced earned income ([Census P60-282, 2024](https://www2.census.gov/library/publications/2024/demo/p60-282.pdf))
- We anchor to **78%** of 65+ households under $75K — slightly above the all-ages 50% to reflect the lower median income of seniors but below the 90%+ implied by some pension data, to stay conservative.

NOTE on the 50–85 range: ACS publishes 65+ as the canonical senior breakpoint. The 50–64 cohort is also FE-relevant but converts at much lower rates (lower mortality salience, more employer life insurance still active). The table below uses 65+ as the defensible core; the addressable FE pool would be ~1.6–1.8× larger if the 50–64 cohort is added.

## C. Demographics of Final-Expense Buyers

- **Average issue age (policy purchase):** 66.8 years (LIMRA-LIC 2015 data; trended upward over preceding decade as carriers exited the under-50 segment). 2020 data shows average **63.5** for both simplified-issue and guaranteed-issue policies.
  - Source: [LIMRA Final Expense Premium 2015 release](https://www.limra.com/en/newsroom/news-releases/2016/final-expense-life-insurance-premium-improves-5-percent-in-2015/); [LOMA LIC Final Expense Survey 2020 release](https://www.loma.org/en/news/press-releases/2021/life-insurers-council-final-expense-and-preneed-life-insurance-sales-record-mixed-results-in-2020/)

- **Median household income of FE buyers:** ~$24,000 ($2,000/month or less). Industry profile describes "Social Security monthly income of $800–$1,200" as a typical buyer.
  - Source: [Insurance Forums — Ideal FE Demographics thread](https://www.insurance-forums.com/community/threads/what-are-the-most-ideal-demographics-for-final-expense.49387/); [Insurance Geek FE Rates Guide](https://www.insurancegeek.com/life-insurance/rates/final-expense/)

- **Gender split:** Female-skewed. **64% of women** cite "paying for burial expenses" as the top reason they own life insurance vs. **56% of men** (LIMRA Insurance Barometer). Carrier-side data describes the typical FE applicant as "single or widowed female with a high school degree, blue-collar work history."
  - Source: [LIMRA 2024 Insurance Barometer Study](https://www.limra.com/en/research/research-abstracts-public/2024/2024-insurance-barometer-study/); [Insurance Forums — Ideal FE Demographics](https://www.insurance-forums.com/community/threads/what-are-the-most-ideal-demographics-for-final-expense.49387/)

- **Marital status (FE-target):** Concentrated in widowed, divorced, and never-married. ESTIMATE — derived from above industry profile cross-referenced with US Census ACS data showing 32% of women 65+ are widowed and 17% divorced/separated.
  - Source: [ACL 2024 Profile of Older Americans](https://acl.gov/aging-and-disability-in-america/data-and-research/profile-older-americans)

- **Homeownership rate:** **79.0–79.5% of Americans age 65+** are homeowners (highest of any age group). For the lower-income FE-target subset, the rate is somewhat lower (estimated ~70–72%) because owners with mortgage debt and low income overlap heavily with FE buyers.
  - Source: [US Census Housing Vacancy Survey, 2023](https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2023/07/younger-householders-drove-rebound-in-homeownership.html); [Joint Center for Housing Studies — Housing America's Older Adults 2023](https://www.jchs.harvard.edu/sites/default/files/interactive-item/files/Harvard_JCHS_Housing_Americas_Older_Adults_2023_Key_Facts.pdf)

- **Spanish-speaker share / language:** No FE-industry-specific publication breaks this out. Useful proxies: more than 1 in 10 US residents speak Spanish at home; >50% of those have limited English proficiency. Hispanic adults age 65+ rely on Medicare/Medicaid for 82% of medical expenditures vs. 65% for white seniors, suggesting concentrated lower-income status that aligns with FE target demographics.
  - Source: [ASPE Latino Health Coverage Brief](https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/68c78e2fb15209dd191cf9b0b1380fb8/ASPE_Latino_Health_Coverage_IB.pdf); [Commonwealth Fund Hispanic/Latino Coverage, 2024](https://www.commonwealthfund.org/blog/2024/hispaniclatino-adults-lack-adequate-affordable-health-insurance-coverage)

## D. Industry Context Numbers

- **Total US FE policies sold (2024):** **1.06 million policies** (up 10% YoY; LIMRA-LIC survey of 28 carriers — not the entire US market, but the largest published benchmark).
  - Source: [LIMRA-LIC Final Expense Insurance 2024 Release](https://www.limra.com/en/newsroom/news-releases/2025/life-insurers-council-final-expense-insurance-new-annualized-premium-increased-16-in-2024/)

- **Total US FE new annualized premium (2024):** **$1.05 billion** (up 16% YoY).
  - Source: [LIMRA-LIC Final Expense Insurance 2024 Release](https://www.limra.com/en/newsroom/news-releases/2025/life-insurers-council-final-expense-insurance-new-annualized-premium-increased-16-in-2024/)

- **Average face value:**
  - Simplified-issue (85% of sales): **$14,535**
  - Guaranteed-issue (15% of sales): **$9,786**
  - Blended: ~$13,820
  - Source: [LIMRA-LIC Final Expense Insurance 2024 Release](https://www.limra.com/en/newsroom/news-releases/2025/life-insurers-council-final-expense-insurance-new-annualized-premium-increased-16-in-2024/)

- **Average annual premium per policy:** **$990** (calculated: $1.05B ÷ 1.06M policies).

- **Average monthly premium (consumer-facing):** **$30–$70/month** for typical issue ages 60–75; **$45–$150/month** at the wider age band.
  - Source: [NorthStar IA FE Cost Guide, 2025](https://northstaria.com/blog/final-expense-life-insurance-cost/); [ChoiceMutual FE Insurance Guide, 2024](https://choicemutual.com/blog/final-expense-life-insurance/)

- **Distribution channel mix (2024):** Independent agents 86%; affiliated 10%; direct-to-consumer 4%.
  - Source: [LIMRA-LIC Final Expense Insurance 2024 Release](https://www.limra.com/en/newsroom/news-releases/2025/life-insurers-council-final-expense-insurance-new-annualized-premium-increased-16-in-2024/)

- **Average funeral cost (2024 NFDA):**
  - Funeral with burial: **$8,300** (median, includes viewing); $9,995 with vault
  - Funeral with cremation: **$6,280** (median)
  - Direct cremation: **$2,202** (average)
  - Source: [NFDA 2023 General Price List Study](https://nfda.org/news/media-center/nfda-news-releases/id/8134/2023-nfda-general-price-list-study-shows-inflation-increasing-faster-than-the-cost-of-a-funeral)

- **US cremation rate (2024):** **60.5%**, projected to reach 80% by 2035.
  - Source: [US Funerals Online — 2024 Cremation Rate](https://us-funerals.com/2024-us-cremation-rate/)

- **US insurance sales agents (all lines, 2024 BLS):** **568,800** jobs.
  - Source: [BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook — Insurance Sales Agents](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/Sales/Insurance-sales-agents.htm)

- **NIPR Producer Database total entities:** **9.2 million producer records** as of 2025 (covers individuals and licensed business entities across all lines and states; up from 5M+ baseline).
  - Source: [NIPR 2024 Annual Report](https://nipr.com/about-us/news/nipr-releases-2024-annual-report); [Fintech Global on NIPR, April 2026](https://fintech.global/2026/04/17/what-the-latest-data-reveals-about-the-us-insurance-producer-landscape/)

- **Number of US life-insurance-licensed producers:** No NIPR-published single figure isolates "life only" lines of authority. ESTIMATE — using the BLS 568,800 sales-agent figure as a floor (sales-only roles), and noting that Florida alone has 127,351 life & health agents (see section E), a conservative national estimate of life & health producers is **1.0–1.4 million** active licensees.

- **Bombora intent-data scale (2025 reported figures):**
  - **5,000+** B2B sites in cooperative
  - **16.6 billion interactions per month** (~199 billion/year — the "20 billion signals/week" claim does not match the official figure; the accurate figure is ~3.8 billion/week or ~199 billion/year)
  - **4.9 million** unique domains tracked
  - **20,100+** B2B taxonomy topics
  - Source: [Bombora — Our Data](https://bombora.com/our-data/); [Bombora — Co-op](https://bombora.com/co-op/)
  - **Recommendation:** Replace any "20 billion signals/week" copy with "16.6 billion intent interactions per month across 5,000+ B2B sites" or "nearly 200 billion intent signals per year."

- **Florida population age 50–84:** ESTIMATE — Florida 50+ population is approximately 9.5 million using ACS age distribution: 5.09M (65+) + ~4.4M (age 50–64). The 50–64 estimate is derived from Florida's median age of 42.6 and applying the national ACS age-pyramid share (50–64 cohort is ~19% of total population) to FL's 23.5M total.
  - Source: [US Census FL QuickFacts](https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/FL); [FRED FL Median Age 2024](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/release/tables?eid=327082&rid=430)

  - Source: [Census Bureau, 2019–2023 ACS 5-Year Estimates](https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/FL)

- **Florida Medicare beneficiaries (2024):** **~5.0 million** total Medicare enrollees (#2 in US after California). Of those, ~57% are in Medicare Advantage; 1.4M in stand-alone Part D; 2.7M with Part D bundled in MA.
  - Source: [HealthInsurance.org — Medicare in Florida](https://www.healthinsurance.org/medicare/florida/); [KFF Medicare Advantage 2024](https://www.kff.org/medicare/issue-brief/medicare-advantage-in-2024-enrollment-update-and-key-trends/)

ESTIMATE — Florida's share of national FE volume should run **8–10% above population share** because of the older age skew. Florida is 7.0% of US population but 8.0% of US 65+ population; using a 1.15× concentration multiplier (FE skews even older than 65+) puts Florida at roughly **80,000–95,000 FE policies/year** and **$80–$100M in annualized FE premium**, derived from the LIMRA national figures of 1.06M policies / $1.05B premium.
