## Two-Layer Marketing Primer (B2B2C)

Hamilton Marketing Services runs a two-layer model. In **Layer 1 (B2B)**, HMS markets directly to licensed insurance agents to acquire them as paying clients — using targeted Meta ads, intent-based display prospecting, LinkedIn for IMO/agency owners, and Facebook-group organic to reach producers who actively buy leads. In **Layer 2 (B2C)**, once an agent signs up, HMS runs marketing *on the agent's behalf* to the agent's end consumers — the seniors, homeowners, small-business owners, and new drivers who are actively shopping for coverage. Layer 1 turns agents into customers. Layer 2 turns Layer 1 into a recurring revenue engine by delivering exclusive, real-time leads the agent can close. Each vertical in this doc covers both layers: the B2B agent profile tells us *who we're selling to*; the B2C consumer profile tells us *who we're generating leads from* once the agent signs. This is why HMS can charge $750–$1,500/mo retainers and keep agents for 6+ months — we are not a one-trick vendor, we are their full-stack demand generation team.

## Vertical 1 — Final Expense Life Insurance

### B2B Agent Profile
- **Book size:** solo agents or 2–5-person IMOs; typical producing agent writes 4–10 policies/month.
- **Commission:** 80–120% of first-year annual premium for independent agents; 40–70% for captives. Typical $1,200/yr premium = **$660–$1,300 first-year commission, ~$60–$120/yr residual** (5–10% renewal). [Redbird](https://redbirdagents.com/average-final-expense-commission/), [NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/life/learn/life-insurance-agent-commissions)
- **US agent count:** ~2M licensed producers nationally; life-insurance-licensed subset roughly 800K–1M. [NIPR / NAIC](https://nipr.com/licensing-center)
- **Licensing:** state life-insurance license (FL "2-15" Life, Health & Variable Annuity). ~40 hours pre-licensing + state exam.
- **Why they pay:** lead-purchase culture is baked in — this vertical is the #1 lead-buying cohort in insurance.

### B2C Consumer Intent Topics (Bombora-style + search)
1. Burial insurance quote seniors 65+
2. Final expense insurance no medical exam
3. Cost of funeral 2026
4. Whole life insurance for seniors over 70
5. Cheap burial insurance Florida

10. $10,000 burial policy
11. Social Security death benefit enough?
12. Pre-need funeral planning
13. Life insurance with diabetes
14. Simplified issue life insurance
15. Funeral pre-payment plans

### Target Demographic
- **Age:** 50–85 (sweet spot 60–78).
- **HH income:** $20K–$60K.
- **Life-stage:** retired or near-retirement, often on fixed income, Medicare-eligible, may be caregivers for spouse.
- **Psychographics:** watch daytime TV (Wheel of Fortune, local news), low digital confidence but Facebook-active, religious/family-oriented, worried about "being a burden."

### Meta Ad Size Recommendations
- **Facebook Feed 1:1 (1080×1080)** — primary workhorse; older audiences still consume FB Feed heavily. [Ryze](https://www.get-ryze.ai/blog/facebook-ad-sizes-complete-specs-guide-for-2026)
- **Facebook Feed 4:5 (1080×1350)** — maximizes mobile real estate; better stop-rate. [Shopify](https://www.shopify.com/blog/facebook-ad-sizes)
- **Stories 9:16 (1080×1920)** — useful for retargeting warm audiences at lower CPM. Skip for cold — 50-75 demo under-indexes on Stories.
- **Skip Reels** — audience skews too young; spend is wasted.

### DSP Ad Size Recommendations
- **300×250 (Medium Rectangle)** — mandatory; ~40% of Google Display inventory sits in this size and it's the best-performing in-content unit. [Zeely](https://zeely.ai/blog/standard-digital-ad-sizes/)
- **728×90 (Leaderboard)** — above-the-fold desktop; seniors skew higher desktop usage than younger demos.
- **160×600 (Wide Skyscraper)** — sidebar persistence on news/obituary/memorial sites.
- **300×600 (Half Page)** — high-impact, premium impression; runs well on WebMD, AARP, local news.
- **320×50 (Mobile Banner)** — mobile news/weather apps seniors use.
- **Exchange priority:** Google ADX, Yahoo/Verizon, Magnite (premium news inventory where 50+ audiences browse).

### Sample Creative Hook
> "Florida seniors over 50 — lock in a $10,000 burial policy starting at $9/month. No medical exam, no waiting. Protect your family from funeral costs that now average $9,400."

### Seasonality
**None** — year-round demand. Mild lift in Jan (New Year planning) and Oct–Nov (estate/end-of-year planning). This is *the* "always on" vertical.

## Vertical 3 — Home & Flood Insurance

### B2B Agent Profile
- **Book size:** 200–2,000 policies; a single-producer P&C agent typically manages 500–1,200 personal-lines accounts.
- **Commission:** Home **12–18% new / 10–15% renewal**; Flood (NFIP) 15% new, ~15% renewal. [Sonant](https://www.sonant.ai/blog/insurance-agent-commission-structure) On a $3,500 FL premium that's **$420–$630 first-year**, **$350–$525 recurring** per policy.

- **Licensing:** FL 2-20 (General Lines) or 20-44 (Personal Lines). 200-hour pre-licensing for 2-20.
- **Why they pay:** FL insurance crisis has homeowners actively shopping; Citizens depopulation and carrier exits created unprecedented re-shop volume.
